Learn to Whitewater Kayak!

This three day Beginner Whitewater Kayak Course is designed to introduce beginner paddlers to the sport of whitewater kayaking. Our goal is to provide beginner kayaker widh a solid foundation of basic kayaking skills necessary to kayak in class I and II whitewater.

Students will leave the course with a solid understanding of essential kayaking skills such as: ...

  • Outfitting a Kayak
  • Wet exits
  • T-rescues and self-rescue
  • Paddle strokes and bracing techniques
  • Basic reading river and hydrology
  • Eddy-in, peel-outs, and ferrying across the river current
  • Paddling Class I-II Whitewater

    • What do I learn During the Begginer Kayak Course?

    The course start in NIM beach near Rishikesh on the Ganga River. On the first day we introduce our students to kayaking gear and assist them with boat fitting. Then we head to flatwater to learn how to do a wet exit in a whitewater kayak with a spray skirt, and learn self and kayak rescue. We work on the paddle techniques as well as edge control, balancing, we will be also exploring how it feel like to do ferry on a light river current and at the end of the day depending on the progress we might paddle your first ever class I rapid.

    Your Second day we will spent on the same river section as the first day, but we will be working on developing fundamental paddling skills, stronger river ferry's, eddy turns and peel-outs, building underwater confident. On this day we will try out T-rescue as it helps you stay in the boat and makes you more confidental underwater! At the end of our second day we will be running the same rapid, but this time you are not just gonna float down the river but you will be awere of what are you doing and where you are.

    Your third day will be spent on the higer section of the river where we will be paddling class 2/3 Rapids. The section we will decide depending on the group's needs. With a third day of practice, techniques begin to flow naturally as your instructor provides more detailed suggestions and exercises. We believe consecutive days in a kayak assist in knowledge retention, making the third day a turning point for many new kayakers. Our instructors conclude the course with a personal summary of your progression over the three days. They will review skills learned and highlight areas to work on in the future. We will also provide you with resources to continue this incredible sport on your own, including suggestions for gear and lists of local clubs and organizations. This course includes all equipment necessary (unless you want to bring your own), lunch, and transportation.
